# Medicine

## Heart Failure 4 Pillars

1. **ARNI / ACEi / ARB** (↓ preload + ↓ afterload → ↑ cardiac output)
   1. ARNI: `Sacubitril/Valsartan` (↑ BNP → ↑ natriuresis + vasodilation)
   1. ACEi: `Enalapril`, `Captopril` (↓ RAAS activation → ↓ Angiotensin II → ↓ vasoconstriction + ↓ aldosterone)
   1. ARB: `Losartan`, `Valsartan` (↓ RAAS activation → ↓ Angiotensin II → ↓ vasoconstriction + ↓ aldosterone)
1. **β-blockers**: `Bisoprolol`, `Carvedilol`, `Metoprolol` (Block β1 receptors → ↓ sympathetic overactivity → ↓ heart rate + ↓ myocardial oxygen demand → Prevent remodeling → ↑ EF over time)
1. **Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonists (MRA)**: `Spironolactone`, `Eplerenone` (Block aldosterone receptor → ↓ Na⁺ and water retention → ↓ myocardial fibrosis & remodeling)
1. **SGLT2 inhibitors**: `Empagliflozin`, `Dapagliflozin` (Inhibit SGLT2 in proximal tubule → ↑ glucose + Na⁺ excretion → osmotic diuresis → ↓ preload + ↓ cardiac stress; ↓ inflammation; ↓ remodeling)

## 4 Pillars of CKD

1. **RAAS blockade** (ACEi / ARB)
1. **SGLT2 inhibitors**
1. **Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonists** (non-steroidal preferred)
1. **Glycaemic control** (in diabetics)

## DKA Treatment

1. `Fluid`
   - Add `10% glucose` 125 ml/hr when glucose < 14 mmol/L
   1. ~~SBP < 90 mmHg~~
      - Fluid bolus `Normal saline` 500 ml over 10-15 min
      - If SBP < 90 mmHg, Repeat
      - If SPB > 90 mmHg,
   1. _SBP > 90 mmHg_
      - 1 L `Normal saline` over 1 hr
      - Then, 1 L `Normal saline` over 2 hr
      - Then, 1 L `Normal saline` over 4 hr
      - Cautions if kidney or heart failure history present
      - Accurate fluid blanace and aim for urine output ≥ 0.5 ml/kg/hr
1. `Insulin`
   - Fixed Rate Insulin Infusion (FRII) 0.1 unit/kg/hr
   - Continue SC basal insulin analogue if normally taken
   - Assess response hourly
   - Is blood ketones falling ≥ 0.5 mmol/hr:
     - If _Yes_: Continue FRII until **DKA resolves**
     - If ~~No~~: Check infusion pump and adjust FRII
1. `Potassium`
   - Measure venous blood gas urgently; Reassess at 1 hr and 2 hourly thereafter
   - Maintain potassium 4-5 mmol/L
   - If _> 5.5 mmol/L_: No added KCL
   - If ~~< 5.5 mmol/L~~: Add 40 mmol/L (3 G) KCL (premixed)

<details>
<summary>Resolution of DKA</summary>

1. Blood ketones ≤ 0.6 mmol/L AND
1. Venous pH > 7.3 AND
1. Bicarbonate > 15 mmol/L

</details>

### Ongoing Management

- After patient is biochemically stable, and can eat and drink, restore usual insulin regimen (SC injection)
- FRII should be continued for 30 min after short-acting insulin is given at a mealtime
- Review precipitating factors
  1.  Glycaemic control
  1.  Glucose monitoring
  1.  Insulin injection technique & injection sites

## Anaphylaxis Shock

- Release of histamine and other agents →
  1.  Urticaria
  1.  Capillary leak and oedema, (eg. eyelids, lids, tongue, lips)
  1.  Laryngeal oedema → hoarse voice, stridor
  1.  Hypotension
  1.  Wheeze
  1.  Hypoxia and cyanosis
- Different Diagnosis
  1.  Acute asthma
  1.  Hereditary angiodema
  1.  Carcinoid syndrome
  1.  Foreign body inhalation
  1.  Systemic mastocytosis

### Management [#Anaphylaxis]

1. Remove the trigger
1. Call for help
1. Lie flat (if pregnant use left tilt), consider raising legs
1. `Adrenaline IM 0.5 mg (0.5 ml of 1:1000)` anterolateral aspect of the thigh
1. Repeat after 5 minutes if no improvement
1. Airway, Breathing, Circulation
   1. Secure Airway
   1. SpO2 94-98%
   1. 500-1000 mL IV crystalloid (may require repeat)
1. If no improvement after 2 doses of Adrenaline (refractory Anaphylaxis):
   1. `IV Adrenaline infusion (0.05 - 0.1 𝜇g/kg/min)`
   1. Fluid resuscitation
   1. Additional vasopressors
   1. Glucagon if on β-blockers
1. Further management
   1. `Antihistamine` for persistent skin symptoms only
   1. `Steroids` if acute asthma is contributing to the symptoms of anaphylaxis

## Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS)

### ST segment elevation ACS treatment (STEMI)

<details>
<summary>ECG Changes</summary>

- Standard 12-lead ECG to confirming the diagnosis and deciding immediate management
- Repeated ECGs are important
- Earliest change is ST-segement deviation
- In **transmural (full-thickness) MI**, ST-segment elevation + development of Q wave
- In **partial-thickness (subendocardial) MI**, non-ST segement elevation (partial occlusion of major vessel or complete occlusion of a minor vessel)

| Infarction    | Abnormal leads       |
| :------------ | :------------------- |
| Anteroseptal  | V1 to V4             |
| Anterolateral | V4 to V6, aVL, I     |
| Inferior      | II, III, aVF, I, aVL |

</details>

1. Immediate clinical assessment
   1. **12-lead ECG**
   1. **Investigations**: FBC, Urea, Electrolyte, Glucose, Lipids, Troponin
   1. **History** of CVD, Risk factors for IHD
   1. **Examination**: Pulse, BP (both arms), JVP, murmurs, signs of LVF, upper limb pulses, scars from previous cardiac surgery, CXR; Contraindications to PCI or fibrinolysis?
1. Management
   1. `Antiplatelets`
      1. `Aspirin 300 mg` (75 mg x 4)
      1. `Prasugrel 60 mg` (if not high bleeding risk) or `Ticagrelor 180 mg` (if high bleeding risk) or `Clopidogrel 300 mg` (75 mg x 4) (if already taking an oral anticoagulant)
   1. `Analgesia`
      - `Morphine 2.5 - 5 mg IV` + Anti-emetic (eg. Metoclopramide 10 mg IV or Cyclizine 50 mg IV)
   1. `GTN` Sublingual if persistent chest discomfort/pain. IV if persistent symptoms, hypertension, left ventricle dysfunction.
   1. `Oxygen` if SpO2 < 90%
   1. `Anticoagulation`
      1. `Fondaparinux` unless undergoing immediate PCI
      1. `Unfractionated heparin` if radial access for PCI
      1. `bivalirudin` if femoral access
      1. `GP-IIb/IIIa blocker` if angiographically indicated
   1. `β-blockers` (`Metoprolol 5-15 mg IV/50-100 mg PO`)
      1. Start early if no cardiogenic shock or heart block
   1. `ACEi/ARB` when stable

- Presenting within 12 hours from symptom onset?
  - If _Yes_: **if Reperfusion therapy possible within 120 min?**
    - If _Yes_:
      1.  `PCI` + `Glycoprotein IIb/IIIa receptor antagonist (GP-RA)` IV (`tirofiban`, `abciximab`)
      1.  Further management
    - If ~~No~~:
      1. **Fibrinolysis/Thrombolysis IV** (`streptokinase`, `alteplase`) + `LMW heparin` IV
      1. Transfer to PCI center for **Rescue PCI if unsucessful** or **Angiography**
      1. Further management
      1. If ~~No Fibrinolysis/Thrombolysis~~: **NSTEMI treatment**

#### Access Eligibility for Coronary Reperfusion

1. Symptoms
1. ECG criteria
   1. ST elevation > 1 mm in ≥ 2 adjacent limb leads OR > 2 mm in ≥ 2 adjacent chest leads
   1. LBBB (unless prior LBBB). RBBB if ischemic symptoms are present
   1. Posterior changes: ST depression and tall R waves in leads V1 to V3

### Non-ST segment elevation ACS treatment (NSTEMI)

1. ECG & Cardiac monitoring
1. O2 if SaO2 < 90%
1. `Analgesia` (e.g. `Morphine 2.5 - 5 mg IV` + `Metoclopramide 10 mg IV`)
1. `Nitrate` GTN spray or Sublingual
1. `Aspirin 300 mg PO` (Consider second Antiplatelet)
1. Risk assess
   1. Measure troponin
   1. **GRACE** score
   1. Cardiac and other comorbidity
1. For High Risk
   1. Anticoagulant
      1. `Fondaparinux 2.5 mg OD SC` OR
      1. `LMWH` e.g. `Enoxaparin 1 mg/kg/12h SC` until discharge
   1. Second Antiplatelet
      1. `Ticagrelor 180 mg PO` if no PCI and bleeding risk not high
      1. `Clopidogrel 300 mg PO` if no PCI and bleeding risk is high
      1. `Prasugrel 60 mg OD` if undergoing PCI
   1. `Nitrate` if pain continues (eg. `GTN 50 mg in 50 mL 0.9% saline 2-10 mg/hr`: titrate to pain and SBP > 100 mmHg)
   1. `β-blockers` (eg. `Bisoprolol 2.5 mg OD`) in all patients if no contraindications
   1. `ACEi` in all patients if no contraindications
