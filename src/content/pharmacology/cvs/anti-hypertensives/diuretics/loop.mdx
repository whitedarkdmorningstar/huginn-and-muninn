# Loop Diuretics

## Examples

- `furosemide`
- `bumetanide`
- `torsemide`
- `ethacrynic acid` _(non-sulfonamide derivative ‚Äî used if sulfa allergy)_

## Mechanism of Action (MOA)

- Act on the **thick ascending limb of the loop of Henle (TAL)**.
- **Inhibit Na‚Å∫/K‚Å∫/2Cl‚Åª cotransporter (NKCC2)** on the luminal membrane.
- This:
  - ‚Üì Na‚Å∫, K‚Å∫, and Cl‚Åª reabsorption ‚Üí strong natriuresis and diuresis.
  - Abolishes medullary osmotic gradient ‚Üí prevents water reabsorption from the collecting duct.
  - ‚Üë Ca¬≤‚Å∫ and Mg¬≤‚Å∫ excretion (opposite to thiazides).

## Pharmacodynamics (Effects)

- **Powerful diuretics** ‚Äî can excrete up to 20% of filtered Na‚Å∫.
- Rapid and short-acting; effective even in renal impairment.
- **‚Üì Blood volume ‚Üí ‚Üì cardiac output ‚Üí ‚Üì BP.**
- Also cause **venodilation** (especially `furosemide`) ‚Üí ‚Üì preload (useful in acute pulmonary edema).

## Pharmacokinetics

| Property             | Notes                                                                                   |
| :------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Route**            | Oral and IV (IV for emergencies).                                                       |
| **Onset / Duration** | Oral: onset 30-60 min; duration 4-6 h. IV: onset 5 min; duration 2-3 h.                 |
| **Protein binding**  | Highly protein-bound.                                                                   |
| **Elimination**      | Secreted into proximal tubule by organic acid transporter; excreted unchanged in urine. |
| **Renal failure**    | Still effective (unlike thiazides).                                                     |

## Indications

- **Acute pulmonary edema** (first-line, rapid relief).
- **Congestive heart failure (CHF)** with fluid overload.
- **Hypertension** ‚Äî when associated with renal impairment or resistant cases.
- **Acute renal failure** ‚Äî to promote urine flow (convert oliguric to non-oliguric).
- **Hypercalcemia** ‚Äî promotes Ca¬≤‚Å∫ excretion (with IV saline).
- **Cirrhotic or nephrotic edema**.

## Adverse Effects

| Effect                            | Mechanism                                                     |
| :-------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Ototoxicity**                   | Dose-related, esp. with `ethacrynic acid` or aminoglycosides. |

| **Hypocalcemia & Hypomagnesemia** | ‚Üì reabsorption of divalent cations.                           |

## Cautions

- **Elderly** ‚Äî risk of dehydration, electrolyte imbalance.
- **Hypotension / volume depletion.**
- **Diabetics** ‚Äî mild hyperglycemia possible.
- **Concurrent ototoxic drugs** ‚Äî e.g. aminoglycosides, cisplatin.
- **On digoxin** ‚Äî hypokalemia ‚Üë risk of arrhythmia.

## Contraindications

- **Severe hyponatremia / hypokalemia / dehydration.**
- **Hypersensitivity to sulfonamides** (use `ethacrynic acid` instead).
- **Hepatic coma** ‚Äî may worsen encephalopathy due to alkalosis.
